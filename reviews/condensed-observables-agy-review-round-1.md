## General evaluation

This manuscript provides an exceptionally clear, mathematically rigorous, and conceptually precise account of how operator algebras interface with the condensed mathematics program. The author correctly identifies that condensed sets and solidification organize parameter spaces, topological descent, and K-theory, but do not construct physical C*-norms, positivity, or state spaces out of bare algebraic data. The inclusion of Ko Aoki's solidification theorems, including the distinction between connective, complex periodic, and real periodic cases, is accurate and timely. The counterexamples identify common category errors and overclaims.

## Minor findings

1. **Location:** Section 2.1 and Section 3.2.

   **Finding:** The definition of a condensed set provides the equalizer condition for a single surjection $S' \to S$, while the descent theorem uses a finite jointly surjective family $\{S_i \to S\}$.

   **Correction:** Note explicitly that a finite jointly surjective family gives one compact-to-Hausdorff quotient map $\coprod S_i \to S$.

2. **Location:** The objectwise C*-algebra theorem and parameterized quasi-local limit.

   **Finding:** The manuscript correctly relies on nuclearity of $C(S)$.

   **Correction:** State that $C(S) \otimes_{\min} A=C(S) \otimes_{\max} A$, eliminating representation ambiguity for the section algebra.

3. **Location:** Aoki real comparison.

   **Finding:** The real Bott element in degree eight is correct. The mechanism of inversion could be specified.

   **Correction:** Note that inversion uses the module-spectrum structure over $K_{\mathrm{alg}}(\underline{\mathbb R})^{\mathrm{sol}}$.

4. **Location:** Finite parameter example.

   **Finding:** The state-space discussion is correct.

   **Correction:** Explain that $(\lambda,1-\lambda)$ is the classical probability measure absent from the bare family $S\to\operatorname{State}(A)$.

## Conclusion

The theorems are correct, the hypotheses are exactly stated, and the proof architectures are sound.

VERDICT: MINOR REVISIONS
