# Referee report

## Substantive findings

1. **The three arrows and scope.** The separation of the
   microscopic-to-low-energy map, invariant extraction, and lattice
   realization is explicit. The information-loss contract prevents these maps
   from being conflated, and the paper does not claim a general lattice/EFT
   equivalence.
2. **Functional calculus and relative K-theory.** The continuous functional
   calculus statements are sound. The relative difference class correctly
   records the reference and stabilization conventions instead of assigning
   an unsupported absolute label.
3. **Disorder.** The strict C*-spectral-gap construction is cleanly separated
   from mobility-gap claims, which need additional localization estimates.
4. **SSH calculations.** The arbitrary-sign gap, winding orientation, local
   transition charge, and exact staggered-mass detour are correct. In
   particular, negative real inter-cell hopping rotates the loop without
   reversing its orientation.
5. **Discrete winding anti-aliasing.** The revised Appendix C.2 argument is
   correct: from
   $|d\operatorname{Arg}(q)/dk|\leq |t_2|/g_\chi$, the condition
   $h|t_2|/g_\chi<\pi$ ensures that no true inter-sample phase increment
   reaches the principal branch cut. The discrete sum therefore agrees with
   the analytic winding on its stated domain.
6. **BDI interaction boundary.** The Fidkowski-Kitaev reduction is used
   correctly to limit the free classification rather than promote it to a
   universal interacting statement.
7. **Condensed-mathematics interface.** Condensed methods organize parameter
   descent and profinite families without being presented as a source of
   locality estimates, thermodynamic limits, or uniform gaps.

The revised manuscript is clear, human-readable, explicit about proof status,
and consistent with the scope of the Haskell checks.

VERDICT: ACCEPT
