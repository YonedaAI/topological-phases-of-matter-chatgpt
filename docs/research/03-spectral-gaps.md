# Paper 3 Brief: Thermodynamic Spectral Gaps

## Research question

How should a uniformly gapped subfunctor be defined so that finite-volume, ground-band, and GNS gap notions are not conflated, and which known stability theorems apply?

## Strongest defensible target

Define gap witnesses that include state, boundary, exhaustion, and band conventions. Prove only controlled consequences of quasi-adiabatic continuation and established LTQO-based perturbation theorems.

## Dependencies

- Paper 1 locality and thermodynamic dynamics.
- Paper 2 states and GNS representation language.
- A common positive gap witness across parameters.

## Deliverables and gates

- LaTeX paper and current PDF of at least 20 pages.
- Strict Haskell finite-size gap and perturbation experiments.
- Lean finite-spectrum and uniform-witness representation.
- Primary-source audit and AGY publishability review.

## Nonclaims

- Positive finite-volume gaps do not imply a thermodynamic gap.
- Bulk gaps do not imply boundary gaps.
- Spectral flow assumes a uniform isolated band and does not prove one exists.
