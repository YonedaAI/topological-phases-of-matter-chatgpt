# Paper 4 Brief: Microscopic Systems and EFT

## Research question

In which controlled sectors can a microscopic lattice Hamiltonian be compared with an effective field theory or stable topological invariant, and what information is lost?

## Strongest defensible target

Construct and verify the free-fermion map from a gapped self-adjoint Hamiltonian through spectral flattening to a relative operator K-class. Compare selected response theories while recording failures of faithfulness, fullness, and essential surjectivity.

## Dependencies

- Paper 1 locality class.
- Paper 2 functional calculus and K-theory target.
- Paper 3 uniform gap predicate.

## Deliverables and gates

- LaTeX paper and current PDF of at least 20 pages.
- Strict Haskell SSH flattening and winding demonstrations.
- Lean finite spectral-flattening representation.
- Primary-source audit and AGY publishability review.

## Nonclaims

- Spectral flattening is not a general interacting-many-body construction.
- Equality of K-classes is not automatically microscopic phase equivalence.
- A general lattice-to-EFT equivalence remains open.
